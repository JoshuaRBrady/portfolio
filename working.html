How Coding is Like Cooking

  • June 9th, 2014
  • coding
  • 0

“If you learn a recipe, you can cook the recipe. If you learn the technique, you can cook anything!” - Michael Symon

I remember when I first started to cook how important it was to follow a recipe. It didn't seem like my one dish would translate over to anything else either. If I made scrambled eggs, that didn't mean I knew how to make an omelet. So I would practice making scrambled eggs over and over so I could master that one dish. After I felt I had conquered it I could move on to mastering the next dish.

After cooking for so many years now, I realize that mastering one dish at a time is not really how I became good at it. Sure, I like to pick up a specific dish to try over and over again to keep improving it, but that's not what I'm talking about. I found that the real way to elevate cooking was not in a recipe book.
